## 1. Film Recaps: Part One, Part Two, Messiah
Consolidated summaries of the essential story events from Denis Villeneuve’s films and the expected *Dune Messiah* adaptation.

<details>
<summary><strong>Part One</strong></summary>

<a id="part-one"></a>

- **Caladan to Arrakis**: Duke Leto accepts the Emperor’s offer to govern Arrakis, knowing it is a poisoned chalice. Jessica defies Bene Gesserit orders by bearing a son, placing Paul inside a breeding scheme he only half understands.
- **Early prescience**: Paul dreams of Chani and a desert battle where friends die. The dreams feel like branching probabilities, not fixed fate, and are colored by fear and desire.
- **Gom Jabbar test**: Reverend Mother Mohiam tests Paul for humanity. He endures the pain box, proving control over instinct. Jessica and Mohiam argue over the Kwisatz Haderach timetable, hinting the Bene Gesserit no longer own this destiny.
- **Political tripwires**: On arrival, Leto courts the Fremen via Stilgar, while Thufir Hawat maps sabotage left by Harkonnens (booby-trapped ornithopters, doctored carryalls). Liet-Kynes quietly signals Fremen sympathy.
- **Spice harvester rescue**: Paul, Leto, and Kynes witness a sandworm devouring a stranded spice crawler. Paul’s first true spice overload blows open his senses; wind, sand, and prescient fragments flood him.
- **Harkonnen and Sardaukar blade**: Yueh, coerced by the Harkonnens through his wife’s capture, disables shields and drugs Leto. Sardaukar, disguised as Harkonnen troops, lead the assault. Ornithopters burn on the runway; Atreides soldiers are outnumbered and outmaneuvered.
- **Leto’s last play**: Yueh swaps a tooth into Leto’s mouth—a poison-gas suicide chance to kill the Baron. Leto bites the tooth in the Baron’s presence but kills the Mentat Piter instead; the Baron survives.
- **The fall**: Duncan carves a path for Paul and Jessica to flee; Idaho dies in a last stand at the desert outpost. Thufir is captured; Gurney goes to ground. Kynes is mortally wounded and dies alone in the desert, hallucinating ecological plans for Arrakis.
- **Flight into the dunes**: Paul and Jessica crash in a thopter, use the “weirding voice” to escape Harkonnen troopers, and nearly perish to a worm before scrambling to rock. Paul tastes spice in a stillsuit’s reclaimed water, binding him further to Arrakis.
- **Sietch threshold**: Stilgar’s troop surrounds them. Jessica disarms Stilgar to prove value; Paul invokes Fremen prophecy, earning negotiation instead of execution.
- **Duel with Jamis**: Fremen law demands Jamis’ challenge be answered. Paul kills him in his first lethal fight, learning there is no shielded practice on Arrakis. The death binds him to Fremen life; Jamis’ water is claimed for the tribe.
- **Naming and belonging**: Paul receives the sietch name Usul (base of the pillar) and public name Muad’Dib (from the desert mouse). Jessica becomes a Sayyadina-in-training. Chani, assigned as Paul’s guide, quietly recognizes the boy from her own dreams.
- **Vision weight**: In the sietch cavern, spice and exhaustion press Paul into deeper sight. He sees legions chanting his name, holy war spreading like fire, and pathways where refusal is also doom. He resolves to survive, but fears what survival will cost.

</details>

<summary><strong>Part Two</strong></summary>

<a id="part-two"></a>

- **Time jump and raids**: Paul leads guerrilla strikes against Harkonnen spice operations; his Fedaykin grow into a mythic shock force. The Imperium bleeds spice revenue, alarming the Guild and the Emperor. [Early Formation of the Jihad](#interstitial-2)
- **Jessica’s ascension**: To secure Fremen loyalty, Jessica undergoes the Water of Life ritual, transforming the lethal bile into awareness. She becomes Reverend Mother, unlocking Other Memory; her unborn daughter Alia awakens preborn, absorbing countless ancestral voices. [First Water of Life Ceremonies](#interstitial-6)
- **Chani and Paul**: Their bond deepens into partnership. Chani grounds Paul’s humanity and challenges his growing fatalism, insisting on present choices over prophetic surrender.
- **Politics on Giedi Prime**: Feyd-Rautha fights in the gladiatorial arena, displaying charisma and cruelty. Rabban’s brutal governance makes Harkonnens hated, while the Baron plots to replace Rabban with Feyd once Paul is killed.
- **Southern fundamentalists**: Paul and Stilgar court southern Fremen naibs, balancing orthodox religious zeal with pragmatic command. Stilgar begins to see Paul as both messiah and political leader, blurring faith and strategy. [First Rebellions Against Paul](#interstitial-4)
- **Water discipline and training**: Paul teaches new Fedaykin to fight without shields, to move like sand, and to use thumpers and worms as weapons. He emphasizes silence, rhythm, and vision-led tactics, making prescience a battlefield edge.
- **The spice agony for Paul**: Seeking full prescient clarity to counter the Emperor and Guild, Paul drinks the Water of Life. He dies in trance and returns, perceiving time as a lattice. He senses atomic plots, Guild terror, and an unavoidable jihad branching from every path. [First Water of Life Ceremonies](#interstitial-6)
- **Worm mastery**: To unify scattered sietches, Paul publicly rides a great maker. Hooks bite into the worm’s ring segments; Fremen chant his name. The act fuses religion and tactics, proving he can command the desert itself.
- **The Emperor moves**: Shaddam IV brings Sardaukar and the Guild to Arrakis, landing with his court and Princess Irulan to crush the “desert problem.” He underestimates Fremen mobility and the desert’s lethality. [Early Imperial Politics](#interstitial-5)
- **Shield wall breach**: Paul detonates a pre-spice mass (atomics) to shatter the Arrakeen shield wall, a calculated violation of Great Convention framed as necessity. Sandworms surge through the gap, turning the Sardaukar elite into prey. [Immediate Aftermath of Paul’s Victory](#interstitial-1)
- **Battle of Arrakeen**: Fedaykin swarm through dust and storm; air support falls to ground fire. Paul’s prescience lets him anticipate Sardaukar feints. Stilgar commands flanks; Chani fights beside Paul. The Guild watches, fearing spice loss. [Immediate Aftermath of Paul’s Victory](#interstitial-1)
- **Confrontation in the throne room**: Paul faces Shaddam, the Guild Navigator delegation, Reverend Mother Mohiam, and the Baron’s remains of power. He threatens to destroy all spice if denied the throne, holding the Imperium hostage to Arrakis.
- **Kanly duel**: Feyd-Rautha, last Harkonnen heir, duels Paul with hidden poison and cunning footwork. Paul wins, killing Feyd and removing the Harkonnen claim.
- **Political marriage**: Paul demands Irulan as his consort to legitimize rule but pledges his heart to Chani. Irulan becomes a political wedge; Chani feels betrayed by necessity. [Early Imperial Politics](#interstitial-5)
- **Aftermath and dread**: Paul secures the throne; Shaddam is deposed. Fremen celebrate, offworld powers bow. Yet Paul sees rivers of blood in future corridors—his victory births the jihad he failed to avert. Chani walks away, shaken by imperial compromise. [Immediate Aftermath of Paul’s Victory](#interstitial-1) [Birth of the Fremen Empire](#interstitial-8)

<summary><strong>Messiah</strong></summary>

<a id="messiah-film"></a>

- **Setting and cost**: Twelve years of Fremen-led jihad have killed billions, installing Atreides rule across the Imperium. Paul is emperor and messiah, yet hollowed by the violence done in his name. Qizarate priests formalize his myth; Fremen veterans grow into an occupying elite. [Early Formation of the Jihad](#interstitial-2)
- **Political dread**: The Spacing Guild fears monopoly loss if Paul monopolizes spice. The Bene Gesserit resent a rogue Kwisatz Haderach. CHOAM partners grumble under Atreides control. Paul’s prescience shows only painful branches—every path sacrifices something he loves. [Early Imperial Politics](#interstitial-5)
- **The conspiracy’s architecture**: Scytale (Tleilaxu Face Dancer) unites factions: Guild Navigators, Bene Gesserit (via Reverend Mother Mohiam and Irulan), and Tleilaxu Masters. The plot’s lever is psychological destabilization, not battlefield defeat.
- **Hayt arrives**: A Duncan Idaho ghola, named Hayt, is gifted to Paul. He is engineered with buried triggers to assassinate Paul or break him emotionally. Hayt’s presence wounds Paul’s grief, dangling the impossible return of a friend.
- **Stone burner attack**: Conspirators detonate a stone burner in Arrakeen, a nuclear device designed to blind. Paul loses his physical sight; he navigates solely through prescience, deepening his alienation and the theme of vision-as-prison. [First Rebellions Against Paul](#interstitial-4)
- **Chani and Irulan**: Irulan, under BG pressure, doses Chani with contraceptives to force Paul to use her for an heir. Paul refuses Irulan, chooses Chani, and shifts Chani’s diet to Fremen fare, inadvertently accelerating her risky pregnancy. [First Water of Life Ceremonies](#interstitial-6)
- **Paul’s moral spiral**: Blinded yet still seeing futures, Paul is trapped by his own visions. He debates fate with Stilgar, rails against religious excess, and knows the jihad machine cannot be stopped without worse consequences. [Birth of the Fremen Empire](#interstitial-8)
- **Alia’s burden**: Preborn since the womb, Alia is now a young regent figure, battling the voices within. The Baron Harkonnen’s ancestral presence tempts her toward cruelty and manipulation.
- **Guild leverage and spice threat**: The Guild contemplates cutting off spacefolding unless Paul cooperates. Paul counters with the ultimate deterrent: he could destroy spice production entirely, ending all space travel, if pushed.
- **The crisis point**: Chani delivers twins, Leto II and Ghanima, but dies in childbirth. This loss is Paul’s foretold pain. Hayt’s buried memories awaken; Duncan resurfaces, breaking Tleilaxu conditioning through love and loyalty. [First Water of Life Ceremonies](#interstitial-6)
- **Trial and choice**: Paul stands accused by a Fremen fundamentalist (Korba) for betraying the old ways, showing religion has outgrown its maker. He chooses blindness exile over rule, embracing Fremen custom that the blind walk into the desert. [Deep Desert Pilgrimage](#interstitial-7)
- **Endgame**: Paul leaves power to Alia (as regent) and Stilgar/Irulean apparatus, vanishes into the sands, and becomes a living myth. The twins become the seed of the Golden Path—hinting at a future where humanity must be forced to survive beyond prescient control. [Birth of the Fremen Empire](#interstitial-8)

## 2. Experience North Stars & Group Storytelling
Core principles to keep available in every conversation so epic and intimate moments stay balanced and operationally feasible.

### **Epic + Intimate Poles**
- VR superpowers: present the gigantic at true scale and create striking intimacy. Anchor the experience with the swing from “towering sandworm at full presence” to “small sietch chamber, face-to-face with a Reverend Mother and the Gom Jabbar.”

### **Who We Are (Group Storytelling)**
- The audience are lead protagonists; identity must work as a group. Current creative: Gurney Halleck’s surviving Atreides soldiers on a mission to find Paul and Jessica. Possible pivot: align to *Messiah* era as Fremen within the Arrakis guerrilla. Locking a clear group identity unblocks downstream choices (tone, mechanics, casting).

### **Continuous-Time Flow**
- Design as continuous real-time to deepen immersion; avoid hard scene cuts. Only break time diegetically via visions (future/past) to maintain realism while enabling redirection and montage.

### **Epic ↔ Intimate Structure**
- Preferred rhythm: Epic (open desert, worm peril) → Vision bridge → Intimate test (Reverend Mother, Gom Jabbar) → Reorientation → Next challenge.

### **High-Throughput Interaction**
- Interactive design must support throughput: choices that do not stall onboarding/offboarding; mechanics that keep groups moving while reinforcing immersion (e.g., comms system). Vision transitions are justified by spice density and ritual, preserving flow.

## 3. Mechanics & Story Devices

### **Foreshadowing Visions (Echo → Variance)**
- Use visions early to preview future beats (worm-hook catch, Reverend Mother’s guidance, Arrakeen fuse). Mirror key elements later but alter lighting, stakes, or who is present to keep prophecy unstable and agency alive.
- Visions hint at required actions (raise hooks, hold breath, sync steps) without scripting outcomes; players prepare yet must adapt.
- Transitional use: visions justify spatial redirection and temporal montage while preserving diegetic continuous time.

## 4. Narrative Opportunities Between Part Two & Messiah
Curated “experience nodes” between Villeneuve’s *Part Two* and *Messiah*, aligned to epic scale vs. deep intimacy, continuous flow, group identity, diegetic visions, and high-throughput interactivity. Villeneuve ends *Part Two* with Paul seizing the throne, Fremen fanatic devotion, Chani leaving, and a galaxy on the brink of holy war. *Messiah* begins 12 years later after a jihad reshapes the galaxy—leaving a goldmine in the first days, weeks, and months after ascension.

<a id="interstitial-1"></a>

### **1. Immediate Aftermath of Paul’s Victory**
- **Worldstate**: Feyd is dead, the Emperor is deposed, Fremen ecstatic and volatile.
- **Epic**: Smoldering imperial keep; massive Fremen host chanting “Muad’Dib!”; sandworms circling the plateau at night like living mountains.
- **Intimate**: Reverend Mother (Jessica or new) pulls each guest aside for a post-battle “soul reading”: “Do you know the danger of what we have unleashed?”
- **Group identity**: Fremen guerillas who fought with Paul or Atreides soldiers folded into Fremen ranks.
- **Why it works**: Chaotic, politically fragile, perfect for continuous real-time right after the climax.

<a id="interstitial-3"></a>

### **2. Search for Paul and Jessica**
- **Worldstate**: Shortly after conquest, Paul vanishes into deep desert with elite Fedaykin; Jessica disappears into the south for power consolidation/Water of Life/prescient conditioning.
- **Group story**: You are a Fremen strike cell (or Atreides survivors integrated into Fremen) sent to track Paul’s path, check for hunters, and stop assassination attempts.
- **Epic**: Following worm furrows at night; cresting a dune to reveal a sleeping behemoth; nearly swallowed as it stirs.
- **Intimate**: Tiny incense-smoked sietch shrine visions—possible futures of Paul, risk of a messianic empire, war to come, your role in stabilizing or destabilizing it.

<a id="interstitial-6"></a>

### **3. First Water of Life Ceremonies**
- **Worldstate**: Jessica oversees dangerous, hallucinatory rites—prescience-adjacent, deeply intimate, visually spectacular.
- **Epic**: Massive underground cavern filled with chanting Fremen; swelling soundscape of voices merging with desert winds; visions of sandworms becoming “gods of time.”
- **Intimate**: Reverend Mother takes each guest’s hand into shared prescient space; direct eye contact names fear, potential, role. Perfect diegetic trigger for flashforwards/branching while keeping continuous time.

<a id="interstitial-7"></a>

### **4. Deep Desert Pilgrimage**
- **Worldstate**: Paul undertakes deep-desert excursions with select Fedaykin.
- **Epic**: Colossal sandstorm; navigation by Arrakis’ moons; surrounded by four or five worms at once.
- **Intimate**: Quiet, lamplit sietch moment; Reverend Mother confides, “Even Muad’Dib fears what he sees.” Personal vision of averting or amplifying catastrophe—individual arcs without breaking group cohesion.

<a id="interstitial-4"></a>

### **5. First Rebellions Against Paul**
- **Worldstate**: Some sietches reject him politically, religiously, culturally—before the jihad goes galactic.
- **Epic**: Approaching a cave-riddled ridge; echoes of rebellious Fremen chants; standoff between Paul-loyalists and a hardline sietch.
- **Intimate**: In the depths, a Reverend Mother tests: “With the child of prophecy or the old ways?” Perfect for Gom Jabbar or vision corridor.
- **Value**: Moral tension felt in VR—social choice, ritual test, reverence vs. danger.

<a id="interstitial-5"></a>

### **6. Early Imperial Politics**
- **Worldstate**: Landsraad in chaos; Guild Navigators fight for spice quotas; Bene Gesserit emissaries angle for control; Sardaukar remnants attempt guerrilla retaliation.
- **Epic**: Colossal Guild Heighliner hovering over Arrakeen; Fremen sandcrawlers like ants under a celestial hull.
- **Intimate**: BG envoy corners your group—probing intentions, testing loyalty, offering forbidden knowledge through whispered Voice. Ideal spot for a Reverend Mother + Gom Jabbar beat.

<a id="interstitial-2"></a>

### **7. Early Formation of the Jihad**
- **Worldstate**: Militias and offworld fanatics self-organize; Paul never wanted the jihad, but it builds anyway.
- **Epic**: Massive gathering at the Arrakeen flats; early militias rally; sandworms approach; captured Sardaukar are paraded.
- **Intimate**: Bene Gesserit Reverend Mother warns of the tidal wave; private test (Gom Jabbar variant) to see if you will prevent, survive, or be consumed by it.
- **Flow**: Smoke clearing → chanting crowds → private chamber → visions → back to the desert.

<a id="interstitial-8"></a>

### **8. Birth of the Fremen Empire (First Edicts)**
- **Worldstate**: Early decrees abolish select imperial taxes, restructure CHOAM influence, elevate Fremen as warrior elite, and manage spice flow to check the Guild.
- **Epic**: Balcony view of a city illuminated in Fremen blue, celebrating new power.
- **Intimate**: Jessica (or another Reverend Mother) aside: “History turns on fragile hinges. Even now, you may shape it.”

</details>

## 5. Character Groups Who Can Have Visions
Below is a clean, production-grade taxonomy of who the audience could be — meaning what in-world identity gives them the right, ability, or need to receive visions — fully aligned with the era between Villeneuve’s *Part Two* and the events of *Dune Messiah*. Grouped by tone, mysticism level, and how naturally they justify visions as a mechanic for spatial redirection and temporal montage.

### **1. Fremen Acolytes Trained by a Reverend Mother**
- Tone: authentic, spiritual, grounded.
- Vision justification: Bene Gesserit dreamshare/trance; diluted Water of Life; shared glimpses guided by the Reverend Mother.
- Why it works for VR: clear cohort identity; visions double as formal training; seamless “towering sandworm → intimate chamber” shifts.
- Era fit: immediately post-*Part Two* as Jessica consolidates Fremen belief.

### **2. Fedaykin Initiates (Paul’s Elite Desert Commandos)**
- Tone: gritty, martial, mythic.
- Vision justification: trance-conditioning; Paul’s tactical battlefield visions; deep-desert spice saturation.
- Why it works: built-in group storytelling; “tactical” visions contrast with religious tones; training scars/caves justify physical space.
- Era fit: Fedaykin rapidly expand after *Part Two*, bridging into early jihad formation.

### **3. Atreides Survivors Being Trained Into Fremen Ways**
- Tone: transitional, emotional, fish-out-of-water.
- Vision justification: controlled spice doses; Reverend Mother loyalty evaluation; tests to “walk the sand” and survive worm proximity.
- Why it works: visions as tests, not gifts; integrates sandworm encounters and sietch rituals; high empathy for outsiders becoming Fremen.
- Era fit: right after *Part Two*, offworlders are absorbed into Fremen culture.

### **4. The Sietch’s Chosen “Dreamwalkers” (Fremen Mystics)**
- Tone: high mysticism, surreal, poetic.
- Vision justification: spice-smoke inhalation, trance chants, group prescience; Jessica or another Reverend Mother as anchor.
- Why it works: maximal abstract imagery; stylized prescience “rooms”; ideal for VR transitions.
- Era fit: mystics matter during Paul’s early consolidation of power.

### **5. Bene Gesserit Operatives Embedded Among the Fremen**
- Tone: political, conspiratorial, psychologically intimate.
- Vision justification: controlled memory/vision recall; Reverend Mother-triggered shared trances; strategic visions to gauge Muad’Dib’s threat.
- Why it works: natural face-to-face Reverend Mother scenes; internal conflict and secret agendas; deep book precedent.
- Era fit: immediately after Paul’s rise, BG tries to reassert influence.

### **6. A Guild-Sanctioned Spice Exposure Team Studying Fremen Tactics**
- Tone: high sci-fi, slightly alien, epistemic.
- Vision justification: low-level prescience training; raw spice or sandworm ichor exposure triggers unstable visions; positioned as observers of how Paul “sees.”
- Why it works: contrast of imperial tech vs. desert mysticism; visions framed as malfunctions/forbidden insights; tension of “seeing what humans shouldn’t.”
- Era fit: post-*Part Two*, the Guild panics over Paul’s spice control and would send undercover teams.

### **7. Tleilaxu Test Subjects in Psychological Conditioning Trials**
- Tone: strange, unsettling, bio-tech mysticism.
- Vision justification: proto-ghola and sensory conditioning; artificial vision induction to test prescience thresholds; early Duncan Idaho ghola ties.
- Why it works: visions explicitly part of Tleilaxu experimentation; freedom for abstract, stylized imagery; continuous “lab → desert → lab” justified.
- Era fit: Tleilaxu are active in *Messiah* and plausibly begin plotting right after *Part Two*.

### **8. Sietch Orphans or Young Fremen Being Tested for Abilities**
- Tone: emotional, intimate, mythic.
- Vision justification: tests for fear, discipline, prescient sensitivity, and spiritual alignment; Jessica’s Reverend Mother role includes testing the next generation.
- Why it works: strong audience identification; shared trial = shared identity; visions feel like childhood prophecy moments.
- Era fit: consistent with early post-*Part Two* sietch politics.

### **Bonus: People to Whom Paul Has Delivered a Vision**
- Tone: mystical, destiny-laden.
- Vision justification: Paul can push prescient flashes to chosen few—an excuse for real-time “edits” of space.
- Era fit: early post-*Part Two*, before Paul retreats inward; works well if climax involves meeting him.

### **Top 3 Vision-Justified Options for VR**
1) Fremen Acolytes / Atreides Survivors undergoing trials (epic worm → intimate test).
2) Fedaykin Initiates conditioned by Paul’s inner circle (tactical premonition for action/traversal).
3) A Bene Gesserit-linked group receiving controlled rituals (cerebral tone; Gom Jabbar-style encounters).

## 6. Rules of Visions in Dune
A canonical framework for narrative, design, and VR staging.

### **1. Visions Are Probability Maps, Not Prophecies**
- A vision is a high-resolution prediction, not a guarantee; futures branch and shift as choices change.
- Different prescient people see different branching structures; greater prescience distorts the futures being observed.
- Creative implication: visions can be poetic, broken, or contradictory and remain lore-accurate.

### **2. Spice Is the Universal Accelerator of Visions**
- Triggered by inhaling, ingesting, ambient exposure, or proximity to high-spice zones.
- Mild exposure: sensory distortion and intuition; heavy: symbolic visions and emotional futures; extreme: multi-timeline prescience.
- Creative implication: environmental spice density justifies vision transitions for any character group.

### **3. The Bene Gesserit Can Induce and Control Visions**
- Reverend Mothers guide trances, steer which memories or futures are touched, stabilize otherwise overwhelming visions, and can create shared visions.
- Creative implication: a Reverend Mother is the strongest diegetic tool for vision-triggering and spatial redirection.

### **4. Preborn, Kwisatz Haderach, and Gholas Have Special Vision Properties**
- Preborn perceive ancestral memories, see past and future interwoven, and are vulnerable to prescience overload.
- The Kwisatz Haderach (Paul) sees multiple timelines at once, can push visions toward others, and distorts nearby vision fields.
- Gholas (e.g., Duncan Idaho/Hayt) can flash prior lives; visions arrive as psychological ruptures.
- Creative implication: proximity to these beings can justify involuntary or shared visions.

### **5. Visions Can Be Symbolic, Not Literal**
- They are metaphorical, archetypal, filtered by fear and desire, and may show meaning rather than event geometry.
- Creative implication: abstract VR sequences are not only acceptable but expected.

### **6. Visions Can Be False — Depending on the Observer**
- Sources: spice hallucinations, Bene Gesserit conditioning, implanted suggestions, emotional projections, or dreams misread as omens.
- Creative implication: mixing true futures and psychological illusions stays within canon.

### **7. Prescient Visions Are Shaped by the Visionary’s Intent**
- Prescience bends around the viewer’s fear, desire, and role—Herbert’s “vision trap.”
- Wanting a future reinforces that path; fearing a future can strengthen it.
- Creative implication: visions can foreshadow the group’s emotional arc—fear made manifest.

### **8. Shared Visions Are Canonically Possible**
- Water of Life ceremonies or a Reverend Mother can create collective trances, shared futures, and a single “circle of perception.”
- Creative implication: perfect justification for synchronized group-wide visuals in VR.

### **9. The Water of Life Unlocks Deep Prescience**
- Temporarily narrows the doorway of time, lets initiates touch branching futures directly, alters space/motion perception, and is dangerous.
- Creative implication: diluted or symbolic versions can power major vision beats without full ritual peril.

### **10. Encountering a Sandworm Can Trigger Visions**
- Sandworms generate spice and the prescient ecosystem; proximity boosts ambient spice, disorients, and can trigger visions.
- Creative implication: a giant worm beat can naturally precede a vision transition.

### **11. Visions Precede Transformation or Testing**
- Usually arrive before rites of passage, Gom Jabbar-style tests, identity shifts, political turns, or psychological metamorphosis.
- Creative implication: structure segments as Epic → Vision → Intimate Test → Reorientation → Next Challenge.

### **12. Visions Can Skip Time Without Breaking Continuous Real-Time**
- Time outside the vision flows normally; inside, hours or years can be perceived without physical travel.
- Creative implication: ideal for redirecting the audience physically while maintaining diegetic flow.

### **The Golden Rule of Visions in Dune**
- Prescience is perception of possibility, not destiny; fleeing a vision fulfills it, embracing it makes it slip away. This tension drives Dune’s spiritual engine.

</details>
